## Long Description (for the product page)

Running a food truck is hard. You cook, drive, serve, repair, and somehow you're also supposed to manage stock, analyse costs, and figure out why Tuesday at the market was a flop but Saturday at the festival crushed it.

**FoodTracks** is the first SaaS built specifically for food truck operators in France and the UK.

### What it does

**Invoice scanning** — Point your phone at any paper or PDF invoice. FoodTracks extracts every line item automatically: product, quantity, unit price, supplier. No manual entry. No spreadsheets.

**AI sales predictions** — The engine cross-references your historical sales with GPS location, weather forecast, and event type to predict how many portions you'll sell at each upcoming service. Order the right amount. Waste nothing.

**Margin dashboard** — See your food cost, gross margin, and profit per dish in real-time. Know exactly which items are carrying you and which are bleeding money.

**SumUp integration** — Connect your SumUp card terminal in one click. Sales sync automatically, closing the loop between what you sell and what you spend.

### Who it's for

Food truck operators who are tired of losing 200€+ per month to avoidable food waste, spending hours on manual stock entry, or making gut-feel orders that leave them short on busy days or drowning in unsold product on slow ones.

Works for solo operators and multi-truck businesses alike. Mobile-first, offline-ready.

### Pricing

Free plan available — no credit card required. Paid plans start at €29/month.

### Comment 2 — The surprising insight (post ~10:30 AM)

> One thing that surprised us during early testing: food truckers don't struggle with cooking or customer service. The thing that exhausts them is **the admin tail** — invoices, stock counts, cost calculations — all done at night, tired, on a phone.
>
> So we obsessed over mobile UX. Invoice scan to structured data in under 8 seconds on a mid-range Android. That number went through about 30 iterations.

### Comment 3 — Respond to first wave of comments with data (post ~1:00 PM)

> For those asking about accuracy — our invoice OCR + extraction model currently hits **94% field-level accuracy** across French and UK suppliers. The remaining 6% gets flagged for a quick human confirm (one tap).
>
> The sales prediction model uses a 3-factor input: your own historical sales at that GPS zone, weather forecast for the day, and whether there's an event nearby. In A/B testing with beta users, it reduced over-ordering by 31% compared to their previous manual estimates.

### Comment 4 — Address the "why not just use Excel" crowd (post ~3:30 PM)

> @[username who asked about spreadsheets] — totally fair question. Excel works until you're standing next to your truck at 7 PM trying to figure out whether to order 4kg or 6kg of brisket for Saturday. On a phone. While your kids are in the back seat.
>
> The goal isn't to replace a spreadsheet with a fancier spreadsheet. It's to replace the mental load of the decision entirely.

### Comment 5 — Evening energy, forward-looking (post ~6:00 PM)

>
